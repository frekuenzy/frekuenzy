What is Sound Healing?

Sound healing is an ancient practice that uses vibrations (vocal or instrumental-like tuning forks and Tibetan singing bowls) in order to relax your mind and body. In the modern era, we can replicate these pure tones using digital oscillators, like the one provided by Frekuenzy.

The theory behind sound healing is based on the idea that everything in the universe is in a state of vibration, including every cell in your body. When parts of the body become stressed or diseased, they are essentially vibrating at a "disharmonious" frequency. Understanding Brainwave States

One of the most powerful applications of frequency generators is to guide the brain into specific states of consciousness. Here is a breakdown of the primary brainwave frequencies:

  • Delta (0.5 Hz - 4 Hz): Deep sleep, restorative healing, and unconsciousness. This is where physical regeneration happens.
  • Theta (4 Hz - 8 Hz): Deep relaxation, meditation, creativity, and the twilight state between sleep and wakefulness (hypnagogia). The Schumann Resonance (7.83 Hz) falls into this category.
  • Alpha (8 Hz - 14 Hz): Relaxed alertness, calmness, and mental coordination. This is the state of "flow" or "being in the zone."
  • Beta (14 Hz - 30 Hz): Active thinking, problem-solving, focus, and external awareness. High beta can be associated with stress or anxiety.
  • Gamma (30 Hz - 100 Hz): Peak concentration, cognitive enhancement, and information processing.

By using the Frekuenzy generator to play a tone within these ranges (often as a binaural beat carrier or isochronic tone), you can potentially encourage your brain to enter these desirable states.

432 Hz vs 440 Hz: The Great Tuning Debate

Most modern music is tuned to A=440 Hz, a standard established in the mid-20th century. However, many audiophiles and spiritual practitioners advocate for A=432 Hz, often called "Verdi's A."

The Argument for 432 Hz: Proponents claim that 432 Hz is mathematically consistent with the patterns of the universe (such as the Golden Ratio). It is said to be softer, brighter, and clearer than 440 Hz. Listening to 432 Hz music is believed to be more harmonious for the body, causing less listening fatigue.

The Argument for 440 Hz: This is the ISO standard that ensures consistency across musical instruments worldwide. Technically, there is no "wrong" frequency, but they do have different qualities.
