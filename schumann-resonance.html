What is Schumann Resonance?

Schumann Resonance refers to the electromagnetic frequencies generated by the space between the Earth's surface and the ionosphere. The fundamental frequency is 7.83 Hz, often called "Earth's Heartbeat" or the "Earth's natural frequency."

This phenomenon was first predicted by physicist Winfried Otto Schumann in 1952 and confirmed experimentally in 1954. It occurs because lightning discharges create electromagnetic waves that resonate in the cavity between Earth and the ionosphere, producing a constant "hum" at 7.83 Hz and its harmonics.
